The Diffusion of Tea

China, the cultural hearth

Chinese legend has tea as an accidental invention taking place in 2737 BCE when tea leaves flew into mythical Emperor Shennong's boiled water. Hierarchical diffusion is responsible for the following spread of the idea, or at least, the believability of the legend. If just any random person proclaimed their love for an infusion of some strange leaf, nobody would care. But an Emperor is a node of power whose beliefs and ideas can diffuse hierarchically.
Tea became the national drink of China in the Tang Dynasty, 618-906 CE
China is the cultural hearth of tea.

The Diffusion to Japan

Tea was spread to Japan by Japanese monks who went to China to study Zen Buddhism.
